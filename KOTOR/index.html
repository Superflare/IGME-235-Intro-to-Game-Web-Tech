Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic

Publishing Info

  • Published by: LucasArts
  • Developed by: BioWare Corporation
  • Released: July, 2003

Description

Knights of the Old Republic is a role-playing game that uses the Star Wars D20 rule system, which is similar to the 3rd Edition of Dungeons & Dragons. Character development and combat are handled similarly to BioWare's previous RPGs such as the Baldur's Gate series. Combat engine follows similar "real-time with pause" rules. There are only three active combatants on the player's side (as opposed to six in Baldur's Gate). Several characters join the party and can be switched at the player's will.

Throughout the game, the player will visit many Star Wars locations that appeared in the movies, such as Kashyyyk, the homeworld of the Wookiees, the desert planet of Tatooine, and others. Interacting with characters in various ways and performing side quests influences the protagonist's stand with the forces of Light and Darkness. The player can turn the main character into a flawless Jedi, a ruthless Sith, or anything in between. Some of the player's choices influence major events that occur within the storyline.

Game Categories

  • Genre: RPG
  • Perspective: 3rd-person
  • Pacing: Real-time with pause
  • Gameplay: Puzzle and Action elements
  • Setting: Sci-fi
